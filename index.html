Philanthropy & Establishment Funds (PAFs)

Partnerships that sustain grassroots cultural programs.

Establishment

Funds are often established by individuals/families via philanthropic trusts (e.g., Private Ancillary Funds) that manage and invest the money.

Donations & Tax Benefits

Immediate tax deductions for donors; assets usually exempt from income tax.

Grants & Distributions

Funds are distributed as grants to charitable organisations for specific projects/causes.

Key characteristics

  • Discretionary & flexible
  • Support for specific causes (education, arts, disadvantaged)
  • Risk-taking capacity to fund new solutions

How to Receive Funding

  1. Establish Charitable Status (DGR)

    Ensure ATO endorsement as a Deductible Gift Recipient.

  2. Research Opportunities

    Understand grant offerings and guidelines of philanthropic organisations.

  3. Submit Applications

    Clear applications outlining project/cause, outcomes, budget, and evaluation.
