🤝 Philanthropy & Establishment Funds (PAFs)

Partner with us through flexible philanthropic structures that create lasting impact.

Establishment

Funds are often established by individuals or families through philanthropic trusts (e.g., Private Ancillary Funds) which manage and invest the money.

Donations & Tax Benefits

Donors receive immediate tax deductions for their contributions, and assets are usually exempt from income tax — a strategic, efficient way to give.

Grants & Distributions

Funds are distributed as grants to charitable organisations to support specific projects or causes.

Key characteristics

  • Discretionary & flexible: Philanthropic grants can be less restricted than government grants, supporting innovative initiatives.
  • Support for specific causes: Dedicated to areas like education, the arts, or disadvantaged communities for structured, lasting impact.
  • Risk‑taking capacity: Philanthropy can fund new solutions where government may not be positioned to help.

💼 How to Receive Funding

1) Establish Charitable Status (DGR)

Ensure your organisation has ATO endorsement as a Deductible Gift Recipient (DGR).

2) Research Opportunities

Identify grant offerings and application guidelines of philanthropic organisations aligned to your program areas.

3) Submit Applications

Prepare clear, compelling applications outlining your project need, outcomes, budget, and evaluation plan.
